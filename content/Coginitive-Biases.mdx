### **Definition**

A systematic pattern of deviation from rational judgement, whereby individuals interpret information in a way that is influenced by prior beliefs, expectations, emotions, or contextual factors, often leading to perceptual distortion, inaccurate judgement, or illogical inference.

### **My Personal Take**

While general psychology and neuroscience have identified around 180–200 cognitive biases in humans, I have listed a few that I have identified through my own experience and observed others fall into. If one does not actively try to remove these biases, one’s perception of the world can be significantly diminished.
